KENYA SAFARI

Samburu National Reserve

A rugged northern Kenya wilderness known for rare wildlife species, dramatic landscapes, and authentic cultural encounters with the Samburu people.

Overview

Samburu National Reserve is located in northern Kenya along the banks of the Ewaso Ng’iro River. It is famous for its unique “Special Five” wildlife species that are rarely found in other parks.

These include the Grevy’s zebra, reticulated giraffe, Somali ostrich, gerenuk, and Beisa oryx. The park also hosts elephants, lions, leopards, and crocodiles along the riverbanks.

Samburu offers a quieter, less crowded safari experience compared to southern parks, making it perfect for travelers seeking raw wilderness and cultural depth.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Northern Kenya
  • Best Time: June - October
  • Famous For: Special Five
  • Landscape: Semi-arid savannah
  • River: Ewaso Ng’iro
