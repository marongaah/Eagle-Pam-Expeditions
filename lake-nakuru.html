Lake Nakuru National Park

A stunning Rift Valley lake famous for flamingos, rhinos, and rich birdlife diversity.

Overview

Lake Nakuru National Park lies in the heart of Kenya’s Great Rift Valley and is one of the country’s most scenic safari destinations. It is world-renowned for its flamingo populations that once lined the lake shores, creating a pink spectacle visible from afar.

The park is also a major rhino sanctuary, protecting both black and white rhinos in a secure environment. Visitors can also spot lions, leopards, buffalos, giraffes, and over 400 bird species.

With its mix of woodland, grasslands, and lake shores, Lake Nakuru offers one of the most diverse and photographic safari experiences in Kenya.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Rift Valley, Kenya
  • Best Time: Year-round
  • Famous For: Flamingos & Rhinos
  • Wildlife: Big Five (except elephants rare)
  • Activity: Game Drives & Bird Watching
