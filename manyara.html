Lake Manyara National Park

Famous for tree-climbing lions, flamingos, and breathtaking landscapes.

Overview

Lake Manyara National Park is a scenic gem located at the base of the Great Rift Valley escarpment. Despite its compact size, the park boasts remarkable ecological diversity.

It is internationally known for its tree-climbing lions and seasonal flamingo populations that gather along the lake shores.

Visitors can also encounter elephants, hippos, giraffes, zebras, buffaloes, and hundreds of bird species while exploring forests, wetlands, and open plains.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Northern Tanzania
  • Best Time: June - October
  • Famous For: Tree-Climbing Lions
  • Wildlife: Lions & Birdlife
  • Activity: Game Drives
