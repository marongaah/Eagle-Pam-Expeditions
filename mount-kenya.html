Mount Kenya

The sacred peak of Kenya rising through forests, glaciers, and alpine wilderness.

Overview

Mount Kenya is Africa’s second-highest mountain and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, rising dramatically above the central highlands of Kenya.

The mountain is not just a peak—it is a world of its own. As you ascend, the landscape transforms from dense rainforest filled with elephants and colobus monkeys to bamboo forests, alpine moorlands, and finally glacial peaks that touch the clouds.

For climbers and adventurers, Mount Kenya offers one of Africa’s most rewarding trekking experiences, combining raw natural beauty, challenging routes, and breathtaking sunrise views from the summit.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Central Kenya
  • Height: 5,199m
  • Famous For: Glaciers & Hiking
  • Wildlife: Elephants & Buffaloes
  • Activity: Trekking & Climbing
